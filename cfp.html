Scope of the Papers

Internet of Medical Things (IoMT), healthcare cybersecurity, biomedical informatics, and molecular communication and computing for medicine are the focus of the 17th International Symposium on Medical Information and Communication Technology (ISMICT) to be held in Lincoln, Nebraska, USA, on May 10-12, 2023. The theme is ICT in Precision Medicine: Paving the Cyberspace in Diagnostics, Therapeutics, and Drug Development. Papers are invited on topics including, but not limited to, the following:

  • AI/Data Biomedical Analytics
  • Bioinformatics and Systems Biology for Medicine, Healthcare and Welfare
  • Bio/Molecular Communication and Computing for Medicine
  • Body Area Network (BAN) Technologies
  • Communications Systems for Medical Applications
  • Cybersecurity in Healthcare
  • Information Theory and Processing for Biomedicine
  • Internet of Medical Things and Bio-Nano Things
  • Medical Device Regulatory Science
  • Nanocommunications and Nanonetworking for Nanomedicine
  • Privacy and Security Issues
  • Wearable and Implantable Devices
  • Wellness and Sports Training
  • Pervasive Health Care and Patient Monitoring
  • Medical/molecular imaging, PET, CT, MRI
  • Brain machine/computer interface
  • Robotics for surgery operation and rehabilitation
  • Sleep analysis
  • Data science for medical big data

Submission Guidelines

Papers submitted to ISMCIT 2023 must be original, not previously published or accepted for publication elsewhere, and they must not be submitted to any other event or publication during the entire review process. Paper submissions should follow the IEEE template for conferences. A maximum of 6 pages (10-point font) are allowed for each paper, all figures and references included. Submitted papers will undergo a peer review process, coordinated by the Program Committee. Accepted papers will be submitted for inclusion into IEEE Xplore subject to meeting IEEE Xplore's scope and quality requirements.

To submit a paper, please use the instructions here.

IEEE reserves the right to exclude a paper from distribution after the conference if the paper is not presented at the conference.
